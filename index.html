THE PAST

We humans have gone from using rocks to inscribe information to the creation of paper. But the limits to this were evident which then led to the era of apps, the major trend of the previous decade. Yes, the amount of data we could process had improved by leaps and bounds, but it is AI that will take the evolution to the top. The gamechanger of this decade however is AI and AR.

ADVANCEMENTS IN TECHNOLOGY

Artificial intelligence is becoming rapidly embedded in the world around us, often hidden yet ubiquitious. This is called ambient AI and is becoming seamlessly integrated into the fabric of our lives.

The use of voice interface devices for online searches, shopping and other common tasks is always on the rise.

Even before AI's recent rise, computers had already taken over many repetitive tasks that can be described with algorithms. A new phase has begun wherein AI performs far more complex work based on machine learning, including tasks performed unconsciously by humans and not fully described by algorithms. Furthermore, some tasks require more data than human mind can deal with and requires faster speeds than any human could match. For example, automated recognition of speech, handwriting and facial recognition already surpasses human skill. Nor is AI limited to merely replicating what humans do. It exceeds human cognitive abilities in a growing number of applications.

MACHINE LEARNING

Artificial intelligence is an umbrella term encompassing a range of theories and technologies that have existed since mid-twentieth century, undergoing numerous cycles of growth and decline.

The conditions of maruration of AI technology became available at dawn of twenty-first century with evolution of smnaller faster computer processors and a renewed interest in machine learng, a foundational concept central to AI.

There are two aspects to the way we learn: the first being teacher-driver where a pedagogy or structure drives the process, and the second being student-driven where the receiver of knowledge draws from multiple sources. The former is typically bounnd by the limitations of teacher's own knowledge and methods, the latter allows the studens to assimilate far more knowledge from diverse sources, bith organized and unorganized, and develop original fromeworks to become more knowledgeable than the teacher. Both these approaches, one supervised by a teacher of expert and the other unsupervised and self-directed, have their parallels in AI

In conclusion, the idea behind machine learning is to train computers to perform intelligent functions through experience rather than relying on a formal set of instructions. This can lead to discovery of new patterns and relationships.
